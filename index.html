Cab rides in New York City : Uber v/s Yellow Taxis

Uber is a peer-to-peer ridesharing company founded in 2009 whose services and mobile app were launched in 2010 in San Francisco, California. It lets users order a cab at any time of date and requires them to pay in advance as opposed to traditional taxis. Uber is now present in more than 630 cities worldwide and has extended its services to food delivery. In May 2011, Uber was launched in New York City thus becoming a competitor to traditional yellow taxis. Our goal is to explore and analyze the expansion of Uber in this city and provide answers to questions like:

  • How much of a threat has Uber become to yellow taxis? Will it replace them one day?
  • Where and when do people use Uber / yellow taxis most often? Why?
  • More generally, can we use this cab data to understand people’s behaviour and routine?

Thurdays and Fridays

In May 2014 the average number of Uber pickups was around 21.000 pickups per day. The busiest days in May was Thursdays and Fridays sometimes with twice as many pickups than on Mondays. Yellow Taxi pickups was around 476.000 a day.

Busiest hour

Hours

In May 2014 the busiest hours for Uber where around usually around 17 - 19 during the day. But there was more difference in the pickups for yellow taxi. But the peak for Uber was usually between shifts for Yellow Taxi drivers. There was also usually the same lowest pickup rate for both Yellow Taxi and Uber which was during the night.
